
# Social Media

Jim Rohn says:
> You are the average of the 5 people you spend the most time with

As a corollary, I believe that your world view is the average of the media you spend the most time consuming. It's worth considering: what kind of media are you consuming and how is it affecting you?

## Junkfood Media

I have a history of twitter addiction, particularly when I'm sleep deprived. What is it about twitter & similar social media platforms that makes them so addictive?

### The Marketplace of Ideas

The marketplace of ideas that plays out on social media isn't putting the highest price on truthful ideas, it's rewarding virile ones, natural selection style. Those ideas that most effectively spread & stick around in the network's fertile minds are the ones being reborn today in a slightly mutated form.

A few things contribute to the virility of an idea, but two that stand out are danger and empowerment.

#### Danger

**If it bleeds, it leads.**

It makes sense that the human mind is wired to pay attention to dangerous things. Those that aren't got eaten by tigers long long ago. School shootings might be the most prominent example of bloody headlines that attract an obscene amount of attention.

### Editors Considered Harmful

In order to compete within the ruthless marketplace of ideas, media institutions recognize that they need to play the click-bait game to drive attention. But click-bait is so often in poor taste, journalists are trying to build a trusting relationship with their readers. Along comes the editor, the scapegoat who can be blamed by the author for *doing what needs to be done* to their headline.

Similar to the trope of ruthless contractors or a new CEO brought in to a company to help fire a bunch of employees. The existing managers have personal connections with their employees, it's hard to overcome these friendships & start swinging the axe. The outsider doesn't care though, the come in and are willing to play the role of the bad guy to *do what has to be done*. 

I don't like what this market place of ideas is selecting for though, I don't want to be lured into addictive righteous indignation by some dishonest click-bait. And as long as an editor stands between me & the journalist, click-bait headlines will be hard to avoid.
