
# Decentralized Machiavelianism

The policy of the US Gov, in general, is Decentralized Machiavelianism.

The intention is always to do good.

The actual effect of any given policy is to make the problem worse, ensuring that the org responsible for solving it gets more funding & more power
 
## Examples

It looks to me like most policies pushed by the US Gov exhibit a non-zero amount of Decentralized Machiavelianism. But a few concrete examples help to paint the picture:

### Climate Change

The policy in CA is to fight climate change by investing in renewable energy eg solar & wind.

The actual effect is less funding for forest management resulting in more fires resulting in more power/money for those "fighting" climate change.

### Racism

Some policies for fighting racism are CRT + defunding the police.

Less police means more crime means more fear. Add in some CRT to lionize racial identity and we have a reliable recipe for more racism which reliably leads to more power & money for those "fighting" racism.

### Foreign Policy

The policy for stabilizing the middle east includes preventing a military dictatorship.

In reality, a weakly militarized central gov means rebels have a fighting chance so we get groups like ISIS.. And more money & power going to those "stabilizing" the region.

## Strengths

A remarkable feature of Decentralized Machiavelianism Machiavelianism is it's robustness. There is no central coordinator, no single point of failure, nobody at the wheel.

Everyone involved simply does what's in their own self-interest, no secret conspiracy required. The game theory's sound.
